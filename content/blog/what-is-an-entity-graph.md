<p>Schema.org launched in June 2011. Google, Microsoft, and Yahoo agreed on a shared vocabulary for the web — a universal language that would allow any website to communicate its meaning directly to machines, not just humans.</p>

<p>It is now 2026. And the overwhelming majority of websites on the internet either have no schema at all, have schema that is technically broken, or have schema so generic it communicates almost nothing useful to the systems now making the most important decisions about who gets found and who stays invisible.</p>

<p>That gap is not an accident. And closing it is now more important than it has ever been.</p>
